Chapter 3: Epistemic Physics — Fields and Forces of Cognition
3.1 Introduction: From Geometry to Dynamics
In the previous chapter, we established the foundational structure of cognition in Aletheia as a Riemannian belief manifold: a differentiable geometric space where beliefs are represented not as discrete propositions, but as continuous positions embedded in semantic curvature. Geometry, however, is only part of the story. Just as physics requires both a metric and a field, cognition demands not only a topology of meaning, but the forces that shape belief flow.

This chapter introduces the epistemic field dynamics that define Aletheia’s cognitive mechanics. We show how cognitive processes such as attention, learning, coherence, doubt, and insight arise from the interaction of three key scalar and tensor fields: curvature (ρ), entropy (η), and alignment (α). These fields do not merely annotate cognition — they govern it, determining the stability, plausibility, and narrative tension of reasoning trajectories.

We call this framework epistemic physics: a field-theoretic model of belief evolution over a structured manifold of meaning.
